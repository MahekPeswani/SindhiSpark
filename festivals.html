Sindhi Festivals

Cheti Chand

Cheti Chand marks the Sindhi New Year and celebrates the birth of Jhulelal, the revered protector. Devotees perform Baharana Sahib rituals, offer prayers near water, and celebrate with lamps, flowers, and community gatherings.

Teejri

Sindhi women observe Teejri (similar to Karva Chauth) by fasting and praying for their husband's long life. The festival is marked by beautiful mehendi, colorful attire, and rituals under the moonlight.

Diwali

Sindhis celebrate Diwali by lighting lamps, decorating homes, preparing traditional sweets, and worshipping Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity and happiness.

Akhand Jyot

An eternal flame kept burning in Jhulelal temples, symbolizing unending faith, devotion, and continuity of Sindhi spiritual tradition.

Navratri (Garba & Dandiya)

During Navratri, Sindhi communities participate with vibrant Garba and Dandiya dances, praying to Durga Maa and celebrating with colorful community events.

Chaliho Sahib

A 40-day period of fasting and devotion, where followers of Jhulelal observe strict discipline, offer prayers, follow a vegetarian diet, and gather for group prayers and kirtans.
