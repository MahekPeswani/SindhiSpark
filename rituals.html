Sindhi Rituals

Naam Karan (Naming Ceremony)

The baby's name is whispered in the ear by an elder family member while prayers and blessings are offered for the child’s health, success, and prosperity.

Kachi Misri (Engagement Ritual)

Families exchange sweets, clothes, and gifts to formalize the engagement of the bride and groom, symbolizing the sweet beginning of their new relationship.

Palli Pallo (Wedding Ritual)

During the wedding ceremony, the groom’s scarf is tied to the bride’s scarf, symbolizing their lifelong bond, shared responsibilities, and commitment.

Bhaat (Family Gift Ceremony)

The maternal uncle offers gifts, clothes, jewelry, and blessings to the bride or groom, representing the maternal family's love and support.

Janoi / Otho (Thread Ceremony)

In this sacred ritual, young boys wear the sacred thread (Janoi), marking their spiritual growth and commitment to learning and moral life.

Chhati (6th Day Ritual)

On the sixth day after birth, prayers are offered, and the baby is placed in a decorated cradle. The ceremony blesses the child with long life and happiness.

Datar (First Haircut Ceremony)

The baby’s first haircut is performed with prayers and rituals, seeking divine blessings for good health, long life, and a smooth journey ahead.

Kanear (Ear Piercing Ceremony)

Both boys and girls have their ears pierced in a ceremonial way. The ritual is believed to protect from evil and promote good health.

Sakhar Poojan (Sweet Offering)

When the bride enters her new home after marriage, she is offered sugar or sweets, symbolizing sweetness, happiness, and harmony in married life.

Satawara (First Solid Food Ceremony)

The child is given their first solid food — usually sweet rice or kheer — while prayers are offered for the baby’s growth, well-being, and future success.

Ghari Puja (Pre-wedding Prayer)

Held on the wedding day morning, the family performs this special pooja seeking blessings for a happy married life and protection from obstacles.

Vadho / Bhaan (Post-wedding Reception)

After marriage, the couple is formally welcomed by the extended family with blessings, songs, and celebrations, marking their integration into the new family.

Lada Geet (Marriage Songs & Blessings)

During wedding celebrations, women gather to sing traditional Lada Geet — joyful songs that celebrate marriage, family bonds, and cultural values.

Akhand Jyot (Sacred Flame at Weddings)

During weddings, an eternal flame is lit, symbolizing divine presence, purity, and the unbroken blessings of family deities for the new couple.
